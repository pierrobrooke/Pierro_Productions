# Pierro Productions — Brand Guidelines
**The Butterfly System** · v1.0 · Last updated 2026-06-27

---

## 1. Positioning

**Category:** Luxury transformation company

**Visual ratio:** 70% Editorial Butterfly Luxury · 20% Quiet Coastal · 10% Futurist Motion

**Core tension the system holds:**
- Soft + powerful
- Organic + editorial
- Feminine + controlled

**Stance:** Pierro Productions is intentionally feminine-coded by design. This is not a gender-neutral brand and should not be softened toward one. The belief underneath it: these qualities are the ones missing from the culture right now, and naming them clearly is the point.

**Clarissa presents as:** Creative director. Systems thinker. Elegant operator. Someone who turns identity into experience.

**Known risk:** Can tip into "too feminine" if overused as decoration. Mitigation — use feminine *traits* as the working vocabulary (below), not the word "feminine" itself. The visual system should signal it; the copy doesn't need to say it outright.

---

## 2. Brand Voice Pillars

Instead of the word "feminine," the brand runs on these as the missing qualities it's putting back into the room:

1. **Receptivity** — taking in a person or situation fully before rushing to fix it
2. **Intuitive pattern-sensing** — reading what's true before the data catches up
3. **Cyclical thinking** — building in seasons, not just sprints
4. **Relational intelligence** — weighing how a decision lands on people, not only whether it's correct
5. **Embodiment** — trusting the body as a source of information, not an afterthought to the mind
6. **Softness held with edge** — gentle and unshakeable in the same sentence

Every sub-brand should be able to point to at least one of these in its own copy and tone.

## 4. Typography

### Master
- **Headline / display:** Prime Serif (Envato Elements — license required). 18 weights, sculpted serifs, built for luxury branding and editorial headlines.
- **Secondary / tracked uppercase:** Söhne (Klim Type Foundry — paid license).
- **Working substitute until both are licensed:** Fraunces (headline stand-in, free, Google Fonts) + **Switzer** (Söhne stand-in, free, Fontshare). Switzer is the closest free dupe for Söhne's specific warmth once Inter and IBM Plex Sans are excluded — General Sans (also free, Fontshare) is the backup if Switzer doesn't feel right in production.

### Body copy — system-wide
**Switzer**, every brand, no exceptions. This is what keeps five very different headline fonts feeling like one company. Source: `fontshare.com/fonts/switzer`. Free for commercial use (ITF Free Font License).

### Headline fonts — one per sub-brand, all free (Google Fonts unless noted)

| Sub-brand | Headline font | Personality |
|---|---|---|
| Pierro Productions (master) | Prime Serif *(Fraunces as free stand-in)* | Elegant, modern, editorial luxury |
| Cotton Candy Revolt | **Bricolage Grotesque** | Quirky, irregular, pop-with-teeth |
| The Frequency Blueprint | **Fraunces** | Soft, warm, old-style curves |
| Zensual Mastery | **Cormorant** | Intimate, high-contrast, classic — carried over from earlier brand work, kept intentionally |
| Pole Class Pro | **Big Shoulders Display** | Tall, condensed, poster energy |
| Web Development & Design | **Space Grotesk** | Technical, modern, code-adjacent credibility |

No sub-brand shares a headline font with another or with the master. Body stays unified in Switzer throughout.

## 6. Icon & Mark System

Drawn from the place on the wing where the green-dappled scales meet the black-ringed pink. No full butterfly icon, anywhere — abstraction only, per the original brief.

**Primary mark (used everywhere — favicon, app icon, social avatar, header):**
- **Eye Mark** — three concentric rings: a faint outer boundary, a dotted Forest Shade ring (slow 36s rotation), a Veiled Rose ring, and an Electric Orchid core that pulses (scale 1 → 1.25, opacity 1 → 0.7, 3.2s ease-in-out loop). SVG source delivered separately in the icon-concepts file.

**Accent marks (secondary, contextual — used alongside the primary mark, not instead of it):**

| Mark | Assigned to | Where it's used |
|---|---|---|
| Scatter to Solid | Cotton Candy Revolt | Hover states on cards/CTAs |
| Vein Knot | Web Development & Design | Loading states, technical contexts |
| Convergence Dot | Zensual Mastery | Ambient corner mark, page transitions |
| *(none yet)* | The Frequency Blueprint, Pole Class Pro | Eye Mark only for now — open item below |

All marks must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (drop to static, no animation).

## 7. Motion Language

One signature transition. Two everyday micro-interactions. One ambient cursor layer. Two motions reserved for rare, big moments only — kept rare on purpose so they keep their weight.

### Signature transition — used on every page load and every page-to-page nav
**Vein Draw + Pulse** (Clarissa's favorite from the original notes)
- Black ground.
- A single abstracted vein line draws itself: `stroke-dashoffset` 1 → 0, ~450ms, ease-in-out (`cubic-bezier(0.65, 0, 0.35, 1)`)
- Brief hold, ~150ms.
- Electric Orchid pulse at the line's terminus.
- New page fades/reveals underneath, ~300ms.
- Total: ~900ms. Feels editorial, premium — not snappy, not sluggish.

### Ambient, sitewide, always-on
- **Iridescent Edge** — hover states on buttons/cards gain a moving pink → green reflective edge. High-end-fashion feel.
- **Breath Bloom** — hover on images/CTAs: scale 1 → 1.02 and settle. Premium-calm feel.

### Desktop-only ambient layer
- **Flutter Cursor** — tiny wing-shape distortion follows cursor movement, ~3% displacement. Very subtle. Reads as "alive," not gimmicky.

### Reserved for rare, flagship moments only — never the default page nav
- **Wing Reveal** — SVG mask, two abstract wing shapes unfold, new page appears underneath. 600ms, ease-in-out. Save for a launch page or a major reveal.
- **Vein Transition** — pages split like wing veins, content emerges through organic paths. SVG morph + GSAP. "Luxury tech" feeling. Same rule: rare, not routine.
